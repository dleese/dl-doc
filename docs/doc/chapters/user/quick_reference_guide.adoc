= Logipad quick reference guide

This guide provides a quick reference to the main features of Logipad.

== Login and authentication

Each crew member must authenticate individually before using Logipad for operational purposes.
The application starts in an unauthenticated state; no briefing data or documents are accessible until a successful login has been completed.

=== Step 1 — Open the login dialog

[.center-caption]
.Logipad home screen — unauthenticated state
image::./mui/mui_login.png[Logipad login screen,50%,float="center",align="center"]

After launching the Logipad application:

. Verify the status panel (upper-right area of the Home screen) shows *Not signed in* with *Last Success: n/a* and *Last Sync: n/a*.
  This confirms no previous session is active.
. Tap the *Login* button on the left side of the Home screen to open the authentication dialog.

[NOTE]
====
The application version and build date are shown at the bottom of the screen (e.g. *Version: 10.1.7*, *Build Date: 2026-02-23*).
Before logging in, confirm that the installed version matches the version approved for your operation.
Refer to your operator's EFB configuration control documentation.
====

[WARNING]
====
*ARINC 768 compliance — identity and access control:*
Do not proceed with login if the application version displayed does not match the approved baseline recorded in the EFB Configuration Log.
Using an unapproved software version may constitute a deviation from your operator's EFB Programme.
====

=== Step 2 — Enter credentials

[.center-caption]
.Logipad authentication dialog
image::./mui/mui_login_credentials.png[Logipad login credentials,50%,float="center",align="center"]

The authentication dialog is served by the identity provider at *keycloak-cloud.logipad.net*.

. Verify that the dialog title shows *keycloak-cloud.logipad.net* before entering any credentials.
. Enter your *Username or E-Mail* in the first field.
. Enter your *Password* in the second field.
  Use the eye icon on the right to toggle password visibility if required.
. Tap *LOG IN* to submit.

If you have forgotten your password, tap *Forgot Password?* to initiate a self-service reset via your registered e-mail address.

[WARNING]
====
*ARINC 768 compliance — authentication security:*

* Each crew member must log in with their *individual* credentials.
  Sharing credentials between users is prohibited and invalidates the audit trail required for operational accountability.
* Confirm the authentication endpoint URL shown in the dialog title before entering credentials.
  Do not enter credentials if the URL differs from the expected identity provider hostname.
* Passwords must comply with your operator's password policy (minimum length, complexity, expiry interval).
  Do not store passwords in plain text on the device.
====

=== Step 3 — Synchronize after login

[.center-caption]
.Logipad home screen — authenticated and ready to synchronize
image::./mui/mui_logout.png[Logipad post-login screen,80%,float="center",align="center"]

After a successful login:

. The status panel updates to show the authenticated *username* (e.g. *support*).
. Tap the *Synchronize* button on the left side of the screen to pull the latest operational data from the server.
. Wait for the synchronization to complete before using any briefing, documentation, or eForms content.

The *Logout* button is now visible in the status panel for when you need to end the session.

[WARNING]
====
*ARINC 768 compliance — data currency:*

* Do *not* use Logipad for operational flight purposes until at least one successful synchronization has been confirmed after login.
  Operating with data that has not been synchronized may result in outdated briefing packages, documents, or eForms being used in flight.
* The *Last Success* and *Last Sync* timestamps in the status panel must be checked before departure to confirm data currency.
  If either value remains *n/a* after a synchronization attempt, resolve the connectivity issue before proceeding.
====

== Logout

Each crew member must log out at the end of their session to prevent unauthorized access to operational data.

. Tap the *Logout* button in the status panel on the Home screen.
. Verify that the status panel reverts to *Not signed in* confirming the session has been terminated.

[WARNING]
====
*ARINC 768 compliance — session termination:*

* Always log out explicitly when handing over the device or leaving it unattended.
  Do not rely on automatic session expiry as the sole access-control mechanism.
* After logout, confirm that the Home screen returns to the unauthenticated state (Login button visible, status shows *Not signed in*) before leaving the device.
====
